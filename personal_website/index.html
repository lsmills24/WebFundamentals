The Electric Guitar

The first ever electric guitar is attributed to Charlie Christian in 1936 when he attached a “pickup” to his acoustic guitar which he began playing solos with in his band. A pickup is most commonly a series of small steel posts wrapped together with very fine copper wire with a magnet base. This combination allows the transmission and amplification of the vibration of steel strings. The first marketed electric guitars were made in the 1940s by an individual by the name of Leo Fender, the founder of the most famous guitar manufacturing company in the world.

Our Custom Guitars

Here at Mills Brothers Guitars, electrics are our specialty. Leo Fender designed the first solid body electric guitars which were uniquely shaped planks of wood with cutouts for the pickups and electronics along with a bolted-on neck. This began the saga of the greatest instrument in musical history: the Electric Guitar. This is our first custom guitar based off one of Fender's most famous shapes dubbed the "Stratocaster."
